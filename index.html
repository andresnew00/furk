Every day in Georgia, homeless animals are running out of time.

🐾

The shelters are full

Traditional shelters euthanize animals when capacity is reached. Without no-kill rescues, thousands of adoptable dogs and cats in metro Atlanta have nowhere to go.

💊

Medical costs are crushing

Sick and injured animals need surgeries, heartworm treatment, and specialized care that costs thousands per animal. Without consistent funding, even animals who can be saved sometimes aren't.

😔

You want to help, but how?

Most people care deeply about homeless animals. But writing a one-time check and walking away still leaves you wondering: did it really make a difference? Is that animal okay now?

Why Furkids

We rescue the animals other shelters can't save, and we do it with full accountability.

Furkids was founded in 2002 on a simple belief: every animal deserves a chance at a healthy, loved life. Today, we operate the largest cage-free, no-kill shelter in the Southeast, including one of the only facilities in the region dedicated to FIV-positive cats. We don't turn animals away because they're hard to place. We turn them into success stories.

Your questions, answered.

Your monthly gift goes directly to food, shelter, and medical care for homeless animals in the Furkids program. Furkids has been recognized by Atlanta Magazine for having one of the lowest administrative cost percentages of any shelter in the city, which means your dollars work harder here than almost anywhere else.
Yes, absolutely. You can cancel your recurring gift at any time with no hassle and no penalty. Simply email Furkids or call the shelter directly. You are never locked in.
Yes. Furkids is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization (Tax ID 01-0766844), so your monthly gift is fully tax-deductible to the extent allowed by law. You will receive an annual giving summary for your records.
The Sustainer Circle is Furkids' monthly giving program. Members receive exclusive access to insider shelter news, event discounts, and the knowledge that their gift is working every single month to keep animals alive and moving toward their forever homes.
Approximately 1,000 animals are in the Furkids program at any given time, spread across their shelters, 8 PetSmart and Petco adoption centers, and over 400 foster homes in the Atlanta metro area.
Furkids operates the largest cage-free, no-kill shelter in the Southeast and one of the only FIV-positive cat care facilities in the region. They also allow children of all ages to volunteer, something no other metro Atlanta rescue offers. They place more pets per donation dollar than virtually any other organization in the city.
Yes. Furkids rescues animals from overcrowded shelters, hoarding situations, owner surrenders, and their Interstate TransFUR Coalition, which partners with northern shelters to find homes for southern animals. Their blog at furkids.org features regular updates and real stories from the field.
In 2025, Furkids achieved a 97.6% live release rate, meaning that out of every animal that came into their care, 97.6% were adopted, transferred to partner shelters, returned to owners, or placed in foster homes. This is among the highest in the region.
Absolutely. Furkids welcomes volunteers of all ages (including children, which is unique to their program), and their foster network is always looking for people to open their homes temporarily. Visit furkids.org/volunteer to get started.
